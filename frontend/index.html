🎯 How Risk Scores Are Calculated ▼

A district's overall risk profile is computed deterministically through a weighted combination of flood hazard metrics and extreme thermal exposure values:

  • Flood Risk Score (60% weight): Derived from flood rate, rainfall, river discharge averages, water levels above warning levels, and historical flood signals.
  • Heat Risk Score (40% weight): Derived from average temperatures, extreme temperature events, and precipitation deficits.
  • Dual Hazard Escalation: Districts experiencing simultaneous hazard alerts receive an automatic risk index increase.
Overall Risk = (0.6 × Flood Risk) + (0.4 × Heat Risk) + Dual Hazard Adjustment
🚨 Hazard Alert Logic & Threshold Rules ▼

The EOC defines hazard alert tiers based on local physical measurements:

  • Flood Severity Levels:
    • Critical: Water levels exceed danger mark by > 1.5 meters.
    • High: Water levels exceed danger mark by 1.0 to 1.5 meters.
    • Moderate: Water levels exceed danger mark by 0.5 to 1.0 meters.
    • Low: Routine conditions or water level < 0.5 meters.
  • Heat Alert Tiers:
    • Red: Temperature exceeds 32°C WBGT threshold.
    • Orange: Temperature ranges from 30°C to 32°C WBGT.
    • Yellow: Temperature ranges from 28°C to 30°C WBGT.
    • Normal: Temperature < 28°C.
  • Dual Hazard Alerts: Triggered when a district has active alerts in both categories.
⚡ Operational Priority Framework ▼

Operational priority determines EOC resource routing and dispatch hierarchies. It categorizes districts into four priority levels based on overall hazard severity and risk index values:

  • Critical (Priority 1): Overall Risk > 75, or dual hazards present, or critical flood active with limited response team coverage. Immediate action required.
  • High (Priority 2): Overall Risk between 50 and 75, or high flood alerts active. Fast mobilization.
  • Moderate (Priority 3): Overall Risk between 25 and 50, or moderate hazard flags active. Regular patrols.
  • Low (Priority 4): Overall Risk < 25. Routine command center monitoring.
🛠️ Recommended Actions Framework ▼

The EOC decision support engine translates incoming threat metrics into standard operating procedures (SOPs):

  • Critical Flood: deploy additional response teams, initialize shoreline barrier deployments.
  • High Water Level: activate evacuation readiness protocols, coordinate local shelters.
  • Dual Hazard: initialize multi-agency emergency operations (coordinate flood rescue + heat shelters).
  • No Response Teams in Hotspots: prioritize instant team dispatch to avoid coverage gaps.
